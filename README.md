# SentimentAnalysis

This project investigates the predictive power of social media (3M Tweets) and news (11k headlines) sentiment on stock price movements for five major technology firms (Apple, Amazon, Google, Microsoft, and Tesla) over the period January 2015 to December 2019. We compute sentiment using lexicon-based approaches (VADER, Bag of Words, TextBlob) and pre-trained language models (FinBERT, RoBERTa), and feed it into a Bayesian state-space model (HD-SURDLM following Shao et al. (2025)) benchmarked against machine learning models (Random Forest, LSTM, CNN-LSTM). HD-SURDLM outperforms ML/DL and naive benchmarks on hit-rate, but fails to beat an always-up baseline on recall and F1, suggesting limited sentiment edge during this bull market period. However, a long-only trading backtest based on the model's predictions still achieves a higher Sharpe and PnL than simple buy-and-hold. Limitations include uneven headline coverage across firms despite various scraping methods and the lack of follower-level data for tweet weighting.
